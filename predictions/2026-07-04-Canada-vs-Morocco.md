## The call (direction — this is what carries confidence)

- **Winner / advances:** Morocco — a **slight-to-moderate lean**, **confidence: low-medium**. Opta gives Morocco 52.7% in 90 / Canada 21.7% / a 25.6% draw, and ~76% vs ~29% to advance. A real edge, but *not* dominant — this is coin-flip-adjacent, so **Canada's ~29% path and the extra-time risk are named as primary, not footnotes**.
- **Game shape:** Tight, tilted low — a Morocco-controlled game rather than an open one. Not a blowout; a comfortable 2+ goal Morocco regulation win is *not* the modal case.
- **Both teams to score:** **Lean yes, not a lock** (Morocco clean sheet a live secondary). Morocco are very likely to score (Saïbari, Hakimi, Díaz against a Koné-less Canada and an MLS-level keeper). Canada's goal is **earned and live but contingent** — Jonathan David running behind Morocco's *high* line is a genuine route (different from a deep block), but it's tempered by Koné's absence (degraded transitions), Davies starting yet not fully fit (their pace weapon blunted), and Morocco's smothering control (they held the Netherlands to 0.33 xG over 120 minutes). Not a Ghana-style suppression, not a gift.
- **Extra-time / penalty risk (knockout):** **High — a primary path** (Opta ET 25.6%, nearly as likely as a Canada win). If it reaches a shootout, **Bounou is the single most asymmetric factor** — he saved two penalties vs Spain in 2022 and the decisive one vs the Netherlands in this tournament, so Morocco's advance probability sits *above* the raw 90-minute number; Crépeau has no comparable pedigree.

## Why (2–4 sentences)

Two sides separated by a real but modest gap (FIFA 6 vs 30; ~57% Morocco in 90) is a coin flip with a lean, so the honest call is a low-medium Morocco edge, not a confident winner. Morocco are the better, more organised side — they smothered the Netherlands and carry a proven shootout keeper — but they aren't a wall (three conceded in the group), so Canada's transition threat is real, not a consolation. The swing is Canada's engine: Koné is out and Davies is only part-fit, which blunts exactly the pace-in-behind that Canada relies on — and Hakimi attacks that same weakened left flank. With both defences live and a 25.6% draw probability, extra time and penalties are a primary slice of the outcome, and that's where Bounou tilts it back to Morocco.
