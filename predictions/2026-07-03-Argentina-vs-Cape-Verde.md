## Why (2–4 sentences)

Argentina are a decisive favourite — reigning champions, Messi in the form of his life (6 group goals, chasing the all-time World Cup record), an elite midfield, and zero goals conceded in the group — so the direction is high-confidence, not hedged. But Cape Verde are a genuine hard block, not a soft one: they shut out Spain, kept two clean sheets, and their 40-year-old keeper Vozinha is fit and starting, so the honest margin is narrow and the temptation to inflate the scoreline must be resisted. Their attack, however, is the flip side of that discipline — no open-play goal all tournament and just 0.30 xG against a comparable press — so their goal is a specific-route possibility (set-piece, long-range, error), not something to hand them for balance. The only realistic upset path is a 0-0 grind into a shootout, and even there Emiliano Martinez tilts the odds back to Argentina.
