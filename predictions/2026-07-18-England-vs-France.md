## Why (2–4 sentences)

A bronze final is the one fixture where motivation and rotation dominate talent, and here the ~even talent picture (FIFA #3 vs #4, a near-even market) is broken only by asymmetric incentive: France's Golden-Boot-and-farewell pull toward a serious XI against England's why-are-we-here indifference. Goals should come — third-place games run hot historically (~3.8 g/g as defensive intensity drops), France's back line is downgraded with Saliba out injured, and both keepers could be backups — but the Miami heat (~93°F, heat index 100-106°F) is a real second-half tempo suppressant that argues against a full shootout. The single biggest caveat is that this entire read is built on **unconfirmed rotation** 2.5 days out: if England field their first-choice attack, the France lean evaporates. One soft, unweighted factor considered and set aside: England arguably carry the more painful semifinal exit (a 2-1 stoppage-time collapse to Argentina vs France's cleaner 0-2 to Spain), which — if anything — nudges the same way (England likelier to come out flat), but emotional hangover is too unreliable to lean on.
